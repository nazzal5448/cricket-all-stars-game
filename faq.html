Game and Features

Questions about what is in the game.

Cricket All Stars was developed and published by KRAFTON, Inc. — the South Korean gaming studio that also made PUBG: Battlegrounds and Battlegrounds Mobile India (BGMI). This is their second cricket game after taking over publishing rights for Real Cricket in 2024.
In early access, two officially licensed IPL teams are available: Kolkata Knight Riders (KKR) and Lucknow Super Giants (LSG). Both include accurate jerseys, real player names, and official player likenesses. More IPL franchises are expected once the game exits early access and launches fully.
Five modes are available in early access: Quick Match (short format, no setup), Friendly Match (play against a friend), Test Match (five-day format), Indian Pro T20 League (full IPL season, offline), and 20-Over World Cup (international T20 format, offline). Additional modes confirmed for later updates include an ODI World Cup Tournament, a Winners Trophy, and a USA T20 League.

Gameplay

Questions about how to play.

Batting in Cricket All Stars uses a tap system. You watch the delivery, read the line and length from the trajectory preview, and tap in the direction you want to hit. Timing is key — tapping early gets you drives, tapping on time gets pulls and hooks, and tapping late results in edges or mistimed shots. There is no swipe mode by default. Check our gameplay tips page for a full batting breakdown.
Start at Semi-Pro. Amateur is too easy and gets boring fast. Semi-Pro gives you a real challenge that teaches the game without being punishing. Move up to Professional once you are consistently winning at Semi-Pro. Legendary difficulty is for experienced players who want a serious test.
